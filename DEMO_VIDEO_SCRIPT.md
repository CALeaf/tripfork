# TripFork demo video plan

Target length: **2:35–2:45**. The final upload must stay under three minutes.

## Submission requirements checked

- Category: **Apps for Your Life**.
- Upload a **public YouTube video shorter than three minutes**.
- Show the working product and use audio to explain how **Codex and GPT-5.6** were used.
- Submit the project description, public repository, and a README with setup and sample-data instructions.
- Explain where Codex accelerated the work, the important product decisions, and how GPT-5.6 works inside the product.
- Include the `/feedback` Codex Session ID from the task where most core functionality was built.
- Deadline: **July 21, 2026 at 5:00 PM PDT**.

Sources: [OpenAI Build Week](https://openai.com/build-week/) and the [official challenge page linked by OpenAI](https://openai.devpost.com/).

## Recording setup

- Record in English because the judging materials are in English.
- Use a 16:9 canvas, 1080p, with browser zoom around 90% so the comparison table fits.
- Keep the cursor slow and deliberate. Cut loading time and dead air.
- Start on the public TripFork home page in a fresh incognito window.
- Keep the GitHub README and the Codex task ready in separate tabs for the final technical section.
- Turn on captions in the YouTube upload.

## Shot-by-shot script

### 0:00–0:14 — The problem

**Screen:** TripFork hero and the 60-second trial section.

**Voice:**

> Travel planning is not one itinerary-generation problem. A permit, weather change, or price can create several possible trips—and I kept rebuilding mine from scratch. TripFork compares those futures before you have to choose.

### 0:14–0:30 — Start with a real decision

**Screen:** Click **Start the guided trial**. The real Southwest trip opens.

**Voice:**

> A new user can try the complete product in sixty seconds, with no login and no typing. This is my actual six-day Southwest plan, where winning The Wave lottery changes the route.

### 0:30–0:48 — Compare complete alternatives

**Screen:** Click **Show me the comparison** and pause on the table. Point to cost, pace, total transit, experience coverage, and the green checkmarks.

**Voice:**

> TripFork does not show disconnected suggestions. It compares complete, mapped itineraries side by side—cost, days, driving, fatigue, flexibility, and what each option gives up.

### 0:48–1:02 — Personal priorities

**Screen:** Click **Prioritize saving time**. Let the winning score and recommendation change.

**Voice:**

> There is no universal best trip. When I prioritize time, every feasible route is re-scored and the recommendation changes transparently.

### 1:02–1:18 — Reality changes

**Screen:** Click **I won the permit**. Pause on the updated recommendation and next actions.

**Voice:**

> Then reality changes: I win the permit. TripFork activates the right branch and updates the next actions without erasing the alternatives or rebuilding the whole trip.

### 1:18–1:38 — Make proven travel knowledge reusable

**Screen:** Scroll briefly to a route map, then open a She Leaves Notes guide and click **Fork this trip** or **Make this trip mine**.

**Voice:**

> Travelers can also publish what they actually did—with the real route, cost, lessons, and map. Anyone can fork that guide into editable inputs and adapt it without changing the original.

### 1:38–1:58 — The product loop

**Screen:** Show editable inputs for a few seconds, then return to comparison and the publish button.

**Voice:**

> So the loop is complete: bring an existing plan, compare the meaningful forks, choose and save a branch, travel it, then publish a field-tested starting point for someone else.

### 1:58–2:25 — Codex and GPT-5.6

**Screen:** Show the Codex task briefly, then the README section **How GPT-5.6 is used** and a quick glimpse of the comparison API.

**Voice:**

> I built TripFork with Codex in one continuous product workflow. Codex helped turn my travel stories into structured routes, implement the bilingual interface, maps, persistence, testing, and deployment, while we repeatedly made product decisions from real usage. At runtime, GPT-5.6 runs through the Responses API with Structured Outputs. It separates fixed bookings, movable items, must-haves, constraints, and uncertainty, then returns two or three complete itinerary branches plus recommendations for every possible outcome.

### 2:25–2:42 — Close

**Screen:** Return to the comparison table, then end on the TripFork logo and public URL.

**Voice:**

> TripFork turns travel planning from “give me one answer” into “help me make a resilient decision.” Plan it, compare it, travel it, and let the next person fork it.

## Final upload checklist
